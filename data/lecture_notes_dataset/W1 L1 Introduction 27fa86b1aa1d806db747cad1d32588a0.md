## Data Processing Pipeline

It is a sequence of steps that collects, transforms and delivers data from its raw form to usable outputs for analysis.

<aside>
💡

**Data processing pipeline** is an end-to-end sequence of steps that collects raw data, transforms it, and produces insights by carrying the data through analysis. Data from its raw form to usable observations. 

</aside>

<aside>
📎

An e-commerce company collects customer activity data (page visit, clicks and purchases). 

- collects and cleans the data: often contains duplicates or missing fields
- Data then analysed to understand customer behaviour
- This allows the company to identify popular products
- As a result companies improve their strategies
</aside>

### Data ingestion - data wrangling

<aside>
💡

**Data Ingestion:** process of importing raw data from multiple sources into a storage system or pipeline where it can be processed and analysed.

**Data Wrangling:** process of cleaning, organising and transforming raw data into a format that is ready for analysis.

- This could be through a mixture of manual and automatic processes
    - removing data duplicates or standardising formats
    - correcting typos or filling missing values based on domain knowledge
</aside>

### Analysis and Modelling - Prediction and Optimisation

<aside>
💡

**Analysis and Modelling:** Process of extracting the model from the data. Goal to build a model that can explain the outcomes of your data. 

**Prediction and Optimisation:** process of using the models to forecast future outcomes and also to further refine it to make better predictions. 

</aside>

### Reporting and Visualisation

<aside>
💡

**Reporting and visualisation:** the process of presenting data and analysis results in an understandable way. 

- this process can be performed at every stage
- easier to extract meaning from images than tables of data
- deciding what to report is also important, don’t need every bit of information
</aside>

---

### Editorial Control

<aside>
💡

**Editorial Control:** The process of making deliberate decisions about how data is presented. 

- Involves making choices about what is show, how to show it and why.
</aside>

<aside>
📎

**Key considerations**

- Purpose of visualisation
- Audience: who are we presenting for
    - Can they trust us?
- Evidence: support your claims
</aside>

Good editorial control ensures your visualisation is accurate, relevant, and understandable. 

---

### Data integrity

<aside>
💡

**Data Integrity:** Ensuring data is accurate, consistent and trustworthy throughout its lifecycle. 

</aside>

<aside>
📎

**Key considerations:**

- legal issues: comply with data protection laws and regulations
    - *Data Protection Act 2018* and GDPR - general data protection regulation
    - What you can and can’t do with data.
- Bias: be aware of skewed or unrepresentative data that can affect outcomes
    - In people can influence outcomes
    - In data: address to ensure fair and accurate results
- Ethics: respect privacy, avoid misuse, and ensure fair treatment of data subjects.
    - we are responsible for the data we use
    - Our actions can can affect others using our results
    - Ensure the responsible and ethical use of AI
</aside>
